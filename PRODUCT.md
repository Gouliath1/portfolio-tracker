# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One person: the owner of this portfolio. Reviews holdings at their desk, typically in daylight, as part of a focused financial workflow. Not casual browsing — a deliberate check. Expects data-forward density, not visual spectacle.

## Product Purpose

A personal investment dashboard for tracking stock positions (US and Japanese equities), monitoring P&L in real-time, and reviewing performance over time. Success means: information is instantly readable, data integrity is visible, and the tool gets out of its own way.

## Brand Personality

Precise, measured, quiet.

Not minimal for its own sake — every element is there because it earns its place. Not cold or institutional — it's personal, a tool built by its user for themselves. The voice of someone who reads annual reports for enjoyment.

## Anti-references

- **Crypto dashboard aesthetic**: neon on near-black, electric glow effects, "to the moon" energy. Loud, speculative, reactive.
- **SaaS dark mode with glassmorphism**: blurred glass surfaces everywhere, cyan accent on dark gradient, generic premium-app look. This is where the current design sits and should move away from.
- **Traditional banking / finance**: navy and gold, institutional cold, built for compliance not clarity.

## Design Principles

1. **Data has the floor.** Numbers are the interface. Typography, spacing, and color exist to make data scannable, not to frame it decoratively.
2. **Signal through restraint.** Color only where it carries meaning: green for positive P&L, red for negative, nothing else. A saturated color anywhere else is noise.
3. **Opaque surfaces, real borders.** No glass, no blur, no frosted-over content. Surfaces are what they are. Borders are structural, not decorative.
4. **Density with rhythm.** Information should be dense enough to read at a glance, but spacing should vary enough that the eye finds its own landmarks rather than scanning a uniform grid.
5. **Light is primary.** Checked in daylight, at a desk, as part of a considered workflow. Dark mode exists as an accommodation, not as the character of the product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA compliance. Reduced motion preference should suppress chart animations. Color alone never conveys P&L meaning — sign, arrow, or label must reinforce it.
